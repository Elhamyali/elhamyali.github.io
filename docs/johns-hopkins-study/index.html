What is this study about?

Across the United States, cities, counties, and townships are investing in parks and green spaces as essential infrastructure to improve community health, equity, and climate resilience. But what helps local governments make that happen and what gets in the way?

In this study, we‚Äôre looking at both sides of that question:

  • Internal readiness: how prepared local governments are to deliver equitable parks and green space policies and practices

  • External pressures: the outside factors that influence that work, from funding to political priorities

Local governments are at the center of this story. With control over zoning, land use, and infrastructure investments, they shape how communities adapt to climate risks and who benefits from those decisions. By sharing your experience, you can help identify what internal conditions, partnerships, and resources make equitable action possible.

We‚Äôre also reviewing park and green space policies from cities nationwide to understand how equity shows up in practice and where gaps remain. These insights will guide practical recommendations for how local governments can design and sustain climate-ready, community-centered green space strategies.

This research is part of a doctoral dissertation at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health, led by student researcher Elham Ali, MPH, PMP, under the supervision of Jill Marsteller, PhD, MPP (IRB00034299).
